# How to Play Backstab Court

## What is it?

A debate game where you argue hot takes and an AI jury decides who wins.

Think of it like a party game where your words are your weapons.

---

## The basics

- **Players:** 4 to 6 (bots can fill empty spots)
- **Time:** About 10 minutes per match
- **Rounds:** 4 per match
- **Goal:** End with the most XP

---

## How a round works

### Step 1: The Hot Take appears

A debatable statement shows up on screen. Something like:

> "Crypto is just gambling with extra steps"

One player is randomly chosen as the **Defender**. They must argue this take is RIGHT.

Everyone else becomes **Prosecutors**. They argue it's WRONG.

---

### Step 2: Prosecutors attack (25 seconds)

If you're a prosecutor, write one argument for why the hot take is wrong.

Example: *"Gambling has no underlying technology. Crypto has blockchain, smart contracts, and real utility beyond speculation."*

The better your argument, the more likely the jury sides with you.

---

### Step 3: Defender responds (30 seconds)

The defender sees all the attacks, then writes one argument defending the take.

Example: *"Most crypto buyers don't understand the tech. They buy low, hope it goes up, and sell. That's literally gambling with a tech wrapper."*

---

### Step 4: The AI Jury votes

Five AI personas read everything and each cast a vote:

| Persona | What they care about |
|---------|---------------------|
| Stern Judge | Logic and evidence |
| Drunk Poet | Emotion and creativity |
| Conspiracy Theorist | Original angles and hidden truths |
| Corporate HR | Clarity and professionalism |
| Literal Toddler | Simplicity and gut feeling |

Each one votes:
- **GUILTY** = prosecutors argued better
- **INNOCENT** = defender argued better
- **CHAOTIC** = both sides were equally good (or bad)

You watch their reasoning appear one by one in real time.

---

### Step 5: Verdict and XP

The majority wins.

| Verdict | What happens |
|---------|-------------|
| GUILTY | Defender loses XP, prosecutors gain XP |
| INNOCENT | Defender gains XP, prosecutors lose XP |
| CHAOTIC | Small bonus to everyone |

If the jury can't agree (tight split), an **Appeal** triggers and the stakes double.

---

## After 4 rounds

- The player with the most XP wins
- Titles are awarded:
  - **Most Betrayed** — was on trial the most
  - **Jury's Darling** — won the most defenses
  - **Chaos Agent** — triggered the most appeals
  - **Silver Tongue** — earned the most XP from arguments

---

## Tips to win

1. **Read the personas.** The Stern Judge wants logic. The Drunk Poet wants emotion. Write for your audience.
2. **Be specific.** "That's wrong" loses. "Here's exactly why that's wrong with an example" wins.
3. **As defender, counter the attacks directly.** Don't ignore what prosecutors said.
4. **Short and punchy beats long and rambling.** You have one sentence. Make it count.
5. **The Toddler votes on vibes.** Sometimes being funny wins a swing vote.

---

## The final round

Round 4 is **Double XP**. Everything is worth twice as much. This is where comebacks happen and leads get destroyed.

---

## Playing with bots

Don't have 4 friends online? Click "Fill with bots" in the lobby. Bots have personalities and will argue against you (or defend hot takes) with their own style. They're not pushovers.

---

## What makes this different from other games?

The AI jury isn't one AI making a decision. It runs on **GenLayer's Optimistic Democracy** — multiple independent AI validators each judge your arguments separately, then they must reach consensus. If they disagree, you see the appeal happen live. 

Your words literally go through decentralized AI consensus. That's the game.
